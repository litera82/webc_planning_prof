Job Description

Job Title: Program Manager I –Emergency Ebola Response (Unaccompanied)

Reports To: Country Representative

*This is a short-term, approximately 6 month position

About CRS:

Catholic Relief Services (CRS) is an international non-governmental organization supporting relief and development work in over 90 countries around the world. CRS programs assist persons on the basis of need, regardless of creed, ethnicity or nationality. CRS works through local church and non-church partners to implement its programs.

Background:

An outbreak of the highly lethal Ebola virus disease began in Guinea in December 2013 and continues to increase in Guinea, Liberia and Sierra Leone. Infected travelers spread Ebola to Nigeria and Senegal where small outbreaks developed, but have been contained. The World Health Organization expects transmission to continue for six to nine additional months even in the face of containment efforts, but hopes to prevent further international spread.

In Guinea, CRS is scaling up Ebola response programming via support to Public and Private Health facilities in Macenta, Guéckédou and Conakry including the St. Gabriel dispensary and maternity in Conakry. The main focus of the program is to ensure the continuation of routine services and protection of health care workers in non-Ebola treatment facilities in these locations through the provision of quality and safe routine health care. This includes training of health personnel in appropriate measures for screening, isolation and referral of persons infected with EVD. On the ground Program Management support is needed to ensure timely/quality program implementation, monitoring, reporting and coordination with other actors.

Primary Responsibilities:

The Program Manager will be based within the CRS office in Conakry. The primary responsibility of the PM will be to manage a team dedicated to the Ebola response and facilitate planning of training activities, budgeting, implementation, monitoring, reporting and coordination. The PM will ensure a high level of accountability for CRS resources, as well as identify other funding opportunities that are in line with CRS and the partner response objectives in Liberia.

Specific Responsibilities:

Program Quality and Management

  • Ensure the planning of project activities in coordination with partners including the Ministry of Health, CDC, WHO and the Ebola Coordination Team.
  • Use appropriate project management tools to plan, review and track progress.
  • Facilitate the development and implement appropriate M&E systems using CRS tools and resources to maximize project impact on beneficiaries.
  • Work with trained Quality Assurance Officers to ensure that Infection Controllers are adhering to set safety standard.
  • Assist with the development of new projects or amendments to ongoing programming based on newly identified needs.
  • Maintain up to date documentation related to project approval, monitoring and implementation.

Programmatic Reporting

  • Ensure the timely and quality preparation of donor project narrative reports, quarterly performance indicator tracking tables, baseline and final evaluation reports, as well as other reports needed/required by the local government, donors, the region or headquarters for all programs managed out of Guinea.
  • Support the regular mapping of CRS Ebola activities in Guinea and provide input to situation reports and summaries requested by HQ.

Budgeting, Financial Planning and Compliance

  • Ensure grant management compliance with donor regulation.
  • Ensure the review and timely submission of financial reports by partners working in close coordination with the finance department.
  • Review and analyze budget comparison reports and take necessary steps to ensure proper management and utilization of budgets.
  • Ensure that systems for the management of equipment and local procurement activities are followed for any CRS and Partner activities.

Representation, Coordination, Communication and Security Management

  • Support the Country Program in the coordination of assistance from other sources related to the fight against the Ebola virus.
  • Actively represent CRS as requested in security and coordination meetings as appropriate as directed by the Country Representative.
  • Help with the coordination of visits of CRS, donor and partner staff as needed.

Supervisory Responsibilies: None

Program Manager Competencies:

These are rooted in the mission, values, and principles of CRS and used by each program manager to fulfill his or her responsibilities and to achieve the desired results.

  • Sets clear goals and manages toward them
  • Collaborates effectively with staff and stakeholders
  • Manages financial resources with integrity
  • Applies program quality standards to project design and organizational learning

Agency-wide Competencies (for all CRS Staff)

These are rooted in the mission, values, and principles of CRS and used by each staff member to fulfill his or her responsibilities and to achieve the desired results.

  • Serves with Integrity
  • Models Stewardship
  • Cultivates Constructive Relationships
  • Promotes Learning

Key Working Relationships:

Internal: CR, HoP, Ebola Regional Point person, DRD/PQ, HoOPS, Finance Manager.

External: OCPH (Caritas Guinea), CDC in country team, Ministry of Health and other INGOs working on the Ebola Response, Relevant GOL Departments.

Personal/Professional Skills:

  • Demonstrated strong ability with project grants management, including project design, budget preparation, expenditure tracking, monitoring and evaluation, reporting, etc.
  • Ability to work independently, but also coordinate effectively as part of a team.
  • Ability to lead a multi-cultural team with a high level of respect for local culture.
  • Experience with capacity strengthening and partnership building.
  • Capacity to work closely with, understand, and support local Church partners.
  • Excellent written and spoken communication skills in English. French fluency is required
  • Strong computer skills (MS Word, Excel, Outlook and Power Point).
  • Experience in working with grants funded by OFDA and UN Agencies preferred.
  • Knowledge of CRS and Sphere emergency guidelines.
  • Ability and willingness to work and live in diverse, challenging and potentially unstable environment.

Qualifications:

  • Master’s Degree in international relations, international development or related field.
  • Minimum 3-year experience working within international development, including direct experience in fast paced emergency environments.
  • Professional proficiency in English and French.

Physical Requirements/Environment: The position will be based in Conakry, Guinea where CRS has an office. Travel to partner health facilities with Conakry and other parts of Guinea will be required – following established security and safety protocols.

NOTE: Applications will be accepted until the position is filled.
